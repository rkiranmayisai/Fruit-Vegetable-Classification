Ethylene & Storage Master Guide

🍎
Apples (Ethylene Producers)

Apples release a high volume of ethylene gas. Store them in a separate crisper drawer. Keep away from bananas, broccoli, and leafy greens to prevent premature yellowing.

🍌
Bananas (Ethylene Sensitive & Producers)

Bananas release massive ethylene from their stems. Wrap the crown stems in plastic wrap to extend their life by 3-4 days. Store away from other items unless trying to speed up their ripening.

🍅
Tomatoes (Flavor Preservation)

Never refrigerate fresh tomatoes! Temperatures below 10°C break down flavor membranes and create a mealy texture. Store them stem-side down at room temperature.

🥔
Potatoes & Onions (Do Not Mix!)

Storing potatoes and onions together is a common mistake. Onions emit moisture and gases that trigger rapid sprouting and decay in potatoes. Store in separate cool, dark, dry bins.

AI Harvest Timing Recommendations

Based on our ripeness predictions, here are recommendations for farm inventory and harvesting schedules:

🟢 Stage 1: Unripe Produce

  • Action: Delay harvest. Monitor color index every 48 hours.
  • Logistics: Ideal for long-distance export. Ripening can be triggered upon arrival using ethylene chambers.

🟡 Stage 2: Ripe Produce

  • Action: HARVEST IMMEDIATELY. Pack into ventilated containers.
  • Logistics: Retail-ready. Store in cold storage at 4°C (citrus/apples) or 13°C (bananas) to maximize shelf life.

🔴 Stage 3: Overripe Produce

  • Action: Bypass standard retail channels. Route directly to food processing.
  • Logistics: Process within 24 hours into sauces, dried fruits, purees, or bakery items to capture value.
